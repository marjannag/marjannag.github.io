    About me

    I am a Master of Applied Science and Engineering Candidate at the University of Toronto, and a Junior Fellow at Massey College. I am advised by Dr. Alison Olechowski of Ready Lab at the University of Toronto, and mentored by Dr. Sharon Ferguson of SHARE Lab at the University of Waterloo.

    I graduated top of my class in Industrial Engineering at the University of Toronto, earning a Bachelor's in Applied Science and Engineering (BASc) and academic certificates in (1) Artificial Intelligence and (2) Accounting+Finance. My master's has been supported by the Natural Sciences and Engineering Research Council of Canada (NSERC), and the Ontario Graduate Scholarship. The NSERC Michael Smith Foreign Study Supplement also funded my visiting studentship at Carnegie Mellon University in Summer 2025, where I was advised by Dr. Chris McComb, Director of the Human-AI Design Initiative.

    I conduct research within computer-supported cooperative work (CSCW) and social computing. I use a mix of social science methods and machine learning with digital trace data to (1) study teams interfacing with collaborative tools, (2) inform collaborative tool (re)design, and (3) design automated/algorithmic extensions of collaborative tools.

    More broadly, I am interested in bridging insights from human-computer interaction (HCI) and management to improve collaboration and the strength of intra-team relationships. Thus far, I have used management theory on Psychological Safety and Transformational Leadership to explore HCI questions.

    Past work

    Before my master's, I was a Program Manager at Intel Corporation, leading activities in Engineering, Marketing, and Product for their Open FPGA Stack and Intel FPGA AI Suite programs. My program management experience inspires much of my CSCW research today. My undergraduate thesis, published in the Proceedings of the American Society for Engineering Education, explores motivators of persistence in Computer Science among students whose identities are underrepresented in the field. I remain committed to inclusion and mentorship initiatives as part of my service roles.
